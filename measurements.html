Units of measure

Pressure vs. flow vs. length

The easiest way to be successful when working through calculations is to make sure you're plugging the right numbers into your formula. If you ever get a result that doesn't look right, go back and check to make sure you didn't accidentally plug your value for "flow" into the spot that's supposed to be holding "length". When we're dealing with calculations you should look at the units given for each value:

  • Flow (or volume) is given in gallons per minute (gpm)
  • Pressure is given in pounds per square inch (psi)
  • Length is given in feet. Hand line generally comes in 50 foot lengths while LDH generally comes in 100 foot lengths.
