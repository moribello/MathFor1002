Friction Loss Calculations

Using the friction loss formula

To determine the amount of friction loss in a single line we use the following formula:

FL = CQ2L

Where:

  • FL = the total friction loss in the system (what we're trying to solve for) in pounds per square inch (psi)
  • C = the friction coefficient for a particular size of hose. A chart of common values is available here
  • Q = the amount of water flow, in hundreds of gallons per minute.
  • L = the amount of hose, in hundreds of feet
A word on Q and L

Q and L are based on hundreds of gallons per minute and hundreds of feet, respectively. So,

  • If you were flowing 300 gpm, Q would be 300 gpm / 100 gpm, or 3
  • If you had 1000 feet of large diameter supply hose, L would be 1000 feet / 100 feet, or 10
